Background

The Department of Recreation and Parks manages the City’s extensive network of parks, playgrounds, playing fields, swimming pools, and recreation centers. Baltimore City is proud to feature approximately 6,000 acres of parkland and public space. Within our city borders, there are over 300,000 street trees and a large inventory of park trees. In fact, Baltimore was recently ranked as 15th in the nation by ParkScore for having the most city parks and greenery. The rating system for city parks analyzed 40 of the largest U.S. cities and used mapping technology and demographic data to determine how well each city is meeting the need for parks and green space.

Details

This loan will authorize the Mayor and City Council of Baltimore to borrow up to $8,000,000 for projects such as neighborhood swimming pool renovation, recreation facility expansion and modernization, park rehabilitation, street trees, and improvements to Baltimore’s playgrounds. The Department of Recreation and Parks aims to create 21st century recreation facilities while maintaining approximately 6,000 acres of park land and open space and 300,000+ street trees.

This is a $200,000 capital improvement loan for the Maryland Zoo to improve, repair, and replace facilities. The Maryland Zoo Loan will support projects such as renovations to the Zoo’s aging buildings and the creation of a new penguin exhibit with underwater viewing, “green” design elements, and improved penguin husbandry conditions and water conservation.
